Day 4 - walkout

Milford Track- Day 4: The Walkout

Morning routines and well earned stiffness slow us down. Those twelve steps were a challenge on the knees and we contemplate the flat 13 mile hike out to the boat that would take us to our last lodge. Painkillers, stretching, coffee get us back into the group with poles in hands and packs on our backs.

At this point we have all learned that the sand flies are not to be trifaled with because their un-assuming bites have turned red and extremely itchy. Both Vivian an I woke up trying not to itch. And this area doesn't just have more of these little vampires but the air is cloudy with them. Slathered with bug dope they still find areas not perfectly covered.

We are driven by them because any pause in movement causes a Pigpen like swarm to form around you in seconds.

This day starts off 2 mile down hill that then breaks into a rolling flat terrain. Thankfully this downhill isn't nearly as challenging as the trail leading up to the lodge. The first break is at 5.5 miles at a boat house and then a second lunch spot 4.5 miles at Giants gate falls and then trail wraps up in the a 3 mile segment were we will transferred over 17 people at a time every half an hour to our final lodge in Milford Sound.

Markers pass by and our pace is solid driven by the flies and the unchallenging trail. Most of it is crushed rock few roots and steps but allows us to keep up the speed.

Dechland keeps pace with us for a while and we talk board games. He is a nice Australian who is quite interesting. He has been our most social guide we had on the trip.

At the boat house we dash inside after dropping packs because the flies are extra vicious.

We passed Mackay Falls which is a gorgeous emerald-pooled waterfall. It is said to be the prettiest falls in New Zealand and I would hate to pick favorites but this would be one of the best we had seen over our adventures on these islands.

In and out of dense mossed trees I would not have believed it could get greener from our first day but everywhere was green leafy and the ground was a soft spong-like footing at times. It was a pleasant walk heading in the valley of the pass.

Markers started to blur without picture taken in between as we creeped higher toward thirty. Then we bursted out on to one of the longest suspension bridges and caught view of many of our fellow hikes in the mist wash of Nuku-tau-roa/Giants Gate Falls.

The strong breeze and mist of the falls kept most of the sandflies at bay. I ate the sweets from lunch collection a gluten free coconut chocolate bar and the inner pieces of my left over sandwich. Having done the math knowing we would be forced to wait on rounds of boat crossings while being quickly devoured by the bugs I wanted to make sure I was on the 2nd boat. We had meet up with Troy and Mary and I set what felt like I ke a blistering pace. If my math was right in the next 3 miles we would have to overtake 6 other hikers to make it on the 3 pm boat versus the 3:30. Happily stopping for 4 0person selfies with the mileposts we got to the ferry house and put our names in for slots 28-32. After we had a seat secured so we then strolled to the last sign saying we had made it to the end of the trail at 34.4 miles at s and fly point.

Because independent hikes get priority for the boat the four of us were the last people called to get on the boat a few moments later.

A jumpy splashing ride later were in Milford warf and busses catried us the last 1km to our lodge.

It was a great hike and we all felt sore but accomplished. From our lodge we had crystal clear views of the mountains surrounding the Sound.

That night we had a chance to invite out guides to eat with us and we chatted about many topics but the topic that made Maya daughter of Troy and Maria squimish was all of us taking turns saying the strangest thing we have ever eaten.
