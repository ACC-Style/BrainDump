SECURITY DEPOSIT. Typically, as part of this Agreement, the Landlord would require a payment of a Security Deposit in the amount equivalent to one month's rent, in this case, $1400.00, to ensure the faithful performance of the Tenant under the terms and conditions of this Agreement. This deposit would usually be required upon the execution of the Agreement.

The Landlord and Tenant shall conduct a pre-move-out inspection of the rental property before the Tenant moves out. At this inspection, the Landlord shall inform the Tenant in writing of any necessary repairs or cleaning. The Tenant will have the right to make these repairs at their own expense before the move-out date to avoid deductions from the security deposit.

The Security Deposit is typically returned to the Tenant within 30 days after the end of the Lease Term, less any itemized deductions for cleaning or repairs. If deductions are made, the Landlord provides an itemized statement of these deductions. This Security Deposit is not to be credited towards Rent unless the Landlord gives written consent. Any damage upon move-out deemed the responsibility of the Tenant that exceeds the original Security Deposit of $650.00 will be the responsibility of the Tenant and may be billed separately.

Under Maine law, the location of the Security Deposit must be known to the Tenant, including the name of the financial institution where the funds are being held and the account number.

For this specific lease alteration and change in rental terms, the requirement for a new or increased Security Deposit is partially and previously collected.

PROPERTY MANAGER.

1. Designation as Property Manager

Joseph Marble, henceforth known as the "Property Manager," is designated as the Property Manager for the premises located at THE PROPERTY. This role is effective from "Lease Term".

2. Responsibilities

As the Property Manager, Joseph Marble is responsible for the following:

  • Overseeing and coordinating necessary maintenance and repairs at the premises.
  • Serving as the primary contact for the Landlord regarding any premises-related concerns or issues.
  • Undertaking other responsibilities as care taker of the THE PROPERTY.
  • Optionally: Selling of items from the property left behind by the owner any sale of items left on the property need to be aggreged upon with the Landlord and any revenue from the sale will be shared with the property manager 60/40% 60% to the landlord.
    - Items may include equipment, furniture, building supplies, etc.
    - Items do not include the property, house, appliances or equipment integrated with the house such as generator and/or HVAC.

3. Compensation

In recognition of his services as Property Manager, Joseph Marble shall receive a monthly reduction in his portion of the rent by $100.00.

4. Reimbursements and Costs

Approval of Costs: All costs anticipated to be covered by the Landlord must be pre-approved in writing by the Landlord. However, pre-approval can be waived in emergency situations where a reasonable delay in action is likely to result in further damage to the property, contents within the property, or in extreme cases render the premises uninhabitable. In such emergency cases, the Property Manager is empowered to take necessary action to prevent further damage, provided that:

  • The Property Manager makes a reasonable attempt to contact the Landlord before incurring any costs, and
  • The Property Manager promptly notifies the Landlord of the action taken and the associated costs incurred, furnishing supporting documentation including but not limited to pictures, service provider details, and invoices/receipts.

Reimbursement Method: Upon receiving approval from the Landlord, Joseph Marble may:
- Be reimbursed for any out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the execution of his duties, upon presenting valid receipts to the Landlord.
- Have his rent further reduced to cover the approved costs.
- Have the Landlord directly pay for the approved costs to the service providers.

5. Term and Termination

The Property Manager role shall continue for the term of this agreement, unless earlier terminated by mutual agreement between the Landlord and Joseph Marble. Joseph Marble may resign from the role by providing 30 day notice to the Landlord.

POSSESSION. By taking possession of the Premises, the Tenant(s) acknowledge(s) that they have examined the condition of the property and accepted it in its current state, except as otherwise stated in this Agreement. If the Landlord fails to deliver possession of the Premises at the start of the Lease Term, the Tenant(s) has the option to terminate this Agreement. In such an event where the Landlord does not deliver possession and the Tenant(s) cancels this Agreement, any Security Deposit and any other prepaid rent, fees, including any fees paid during the application process before the execution of this Agreement, shall be returned to the Tenant(s).

ACCESS. Upon the commencement of the Lease Term, the Landlord agrees to provide the Tenant(s) with keys as necessary to access all areas of the Premises. Duplicate copies of the provided keys may only be authorized with the Landlord's consent. If replacements are required, the Landlord may provide them for a fee. At the termination of this Agreement, the Tenant(s) is required to return all keys provided by the Landlord. Failure to do so may result in a fee being charged to the Tenant(s) or the amount being deducted from the Security Deposit.

SUBLETTING. The Tenant(s) shall be able to sublet the Premises.

ABANDONMENT. If the Tenant(s) vacates or abandons the Premises for a period equal to or less than the minimum set by State law or 30 days, whichever is less, the Landlord reserves the right to terminate this Agreement immediately and remove all belongings, including any personal property, from the Premises. In the event of the Tenant(s)'s abandonment of the Premises, the Landlord has the immediate right to terminate this Agreement.

ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING. The Tenant(s) shall not assign this Lease or sublet the Premises without the prior written consent of the Landlord. The consent by the Landlord to one assignment or subletting shall not be deemed to be a consent to any subsequent assignment or subletting.

RIGHT OF ENTRY. The Landlord shall have the right to enter the Premises during normal working hours by providing at least twenty-four (24) hours notice in order to inspect the Premises, make necessary repairs, alterations or improvements, to supply agreed-upon services, or for any reasonable purpose. The Landlord may showcase the Premises to potential buyers, mortgagees, or lessees with reasonable notice. In the event that the Premises are deemed abandoned, compelled by court order, or in case of emergency, the Landlord reserves the right to enter without prior permission.

MAINTENANCE, REPAIRS, AND ALTERATIONS. The Tenant(s) shall maintain the Premises in a clean and sanitary manner at their own expense, and surrender the same at the termination of the lease in as good condition as received, excluding normal wear and tear. The Tenant(s) may not make alterations to the leased Premises without the written consent of the Landlord. The Landlord shall be responsible for repairs to the interior and exterior of the building. If the Premises include appliances such as a washer, dryer, freezer, generator, or heat pump, the Landlord provides no warranty regarding the repair or replacement of these units should they cease to operate.

NOISE AND WASTE. The Tenant(s) agree(s) not to commit waste on the Premises, maintain or allow a nuisance thereon, or use or allow the Premises to be used unlawfully. The Tenant(s) also agree(s) to comply with all local, county, and state noise ordinances.

GUESTS AND SUBLETS. No other persons shall reside on the Premises apart from the Tenant(s), any approved Occupants, and approved sublet tenants. Guests of the Tenant(s) are permitted for periods not exceeding 48 hours unless otherwise approved by the Landlord in writing. The Tenant(s) may sublet the Premises or any part thereof, upon obtaining the prior written consent of the Landlord.

COMPLIANCE WITH LAW. The Tenant(s) agree(s) that during the term of this Agreement, they shall promptly comply with all current and future laws, ordinances, orders, rules, regulations, and requirements of the Federal, State, County, City, and Municipal government or any of their departments, bureaus, boards, commissions, and officials with respect to the Premises or the use or occupancy thereof. This compliance is mandatory whether the order or directive is against the Tenant(s), the Landlord, or both.

DEFAULT. If the Tenant(s) fail(s) to comply with any of the material provisions of this Agreement, or of any present rules and regulations, or those that may be prescribed by the Landlord in the future, or materially fail(s) to comply with any duties imposed on the Tenant(s) by statute or State laws, within a specified time period after delivery of written notice by the Landlord specifying the non-compliance and indicating the Landlord's intention to terminate the Agreement due to this reason, the Landlord may terminate this Agreement.

Should the Tenant(s) fail to pay rent when due and the default continues for the specified time period in the written notice, the Landlord may, at their discretion, declare the entire balance of rent payable under this Agreement to be immediately due and payable. The Landlord may exercise any and all rights and remedies available to them at law or in equity, and may immediately terminate this Agreement.

The Tenant(s) will be in default if: (a) the Tenant(s) does not pay rent or other amounts that are owed; (b) the Tenant(s), their guests, or the Occupant(s) violate this Agreement, rules, or fire, safety, health, or criminal laws, regardless of whether arrest or conviction occurs;

MULTIPLE TENANT OR OCCUPANT(S). Each individual considered as a Tenant is jointly and individually liable for all Lease obligations under this Agreement, including but not limited to the payment of rent. If any Tenant(s), guest, or occupant(s) violates this Agreement, it is considered a violation by all Tenant(s). The Landlord's requests and notices to any Tenant(s) or occupant(s) of legal age constitute notice to all Tenant(s). Notices and requests from any Tenant(s) or occupant(s), including repair requests and entry permissions, represent notice from all Tenant(s). In eviction suits, any Tenant(s) is considered the agent of all for the service of process.

DISPUTES. Should a dispute arise during or after the term of this Agreement between the Landlord and the Tenant(s), they agree to negotiate in "good faith," utilizing clear communication. If disputes persist, the owner will make a decision before any litigation occurs.

SEVERABILITY. If any provision of this Agreement or its application becomes invalid or unenforceable for any reason and to any extent, neither the remainder of this Agreement nor the application of the provision to other persons, entities, or circumstances will be affected thereby. Instead, it shall be enforced to the maximum extent permitted by law.

RETALIATION. The Landlord is prohibited from retaliating against the Tenant(s), which includes but is not limited to restricting access to the Premises, decreasing or canceling services or utilities, failing to repair appliances or fixtures, or engaging in any other unjustifiable act.

WAIVER. A waiver by the Landlord of a breach of any covenant or duty of the Tenant(s) under this Agreement is not a waiver of any other breach of the same or any other covenant or duty. No provision of this Agreement shall be considered waived unless such waiver shall be in writing as a formal amendment to this Agreement, executed by both the Tenant(s) and the Landlord.

EQUAL HOUSING. If the Tenant(s) has any mental or physical impairment, the Landlord shall accommodate reasonable modifications to the Premises unless such modifications would cause undue hardship or require a fundamental alteration of the property. Tenant(s) are encouraged to communicate any impairment(s) to the Landlord in writing to identify appropriate accommodations.

HAZARDOUS MATERIALS. The Tenant(s) agrees not to store on the Premises any materials that are of a dangerous, flammable, or explosive character that might substantially increase the risk of fire or explosion on the Premises or that might be considered hazardous or extra hazardous by any responsible insurance company.

INDEMNIFICATION. The Landlord shall not be liable for any damage or injury to the Tenant(s), or any other person, or to any property occurring on the Premises or any part thereof, or in common areas thereof. The Tenant(s) agrees to hold the Landlord harmless from any claims for damages, no matter how caused, except for injury or damages for which the Landlord is legally responsible.

COVENANTS. The covenants and conditions herein contained apply to and bind the heirs, legal representatives, and permitted assigns of the parties hereto, and all covenants are to be construed as conditions of this Agreement.

PREMISES DEEMED UNINHABITABLE. If the Premises become uninhabitable due to damage beyond reasonable repair, the Tenant(s) may terminate this Agreement via written notice to the Landlord. If such damage was caused by the negligence of the Tenant(s), they shall be held liable to the Landlord for the cost of repairs and for the loss of rental income while the Premises are being repaired, in addition to other provable losses incurred by the Landlord.

GOVERNING LAW. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the state of Maine.

ENTIRE AGREEMENT. This Agreement contains all the terms agreed upon by the parties regarding its subject matter, including any attachments or addenda.
