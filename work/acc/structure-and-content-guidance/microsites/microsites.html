Microsite Definition

A microsite on ACC.org functions like a unique sub-website tailored for a particular purpose or initiative. Think of it as an independent information hub under the main site's umbrella, opening with a vibrant full-page introduction of its theme. Its content, organized in an exclusive structure of related sub-pages, enables users to delve deeper into the specific topic without other site distractions.

Microsites represent a calculated approach for presenting semi-independent projects. While they adhere to most of ACC.org's branding guidelines, their unique design and structure allow them to serve as focused resources for users.

However, creating a microsite is not always advisable due to costs and other considerations. It's generally reserved for significant initiatives that are notably important, brandable, unique, complex, and self-contained. In such instances, a microsite can offer significant value. Keep in mind this is a surface-level explanation.
