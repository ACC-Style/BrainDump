Why use a Micro-Site

A microsite on ACC.org provides a strategic platform for delivering a focused, user-centric experience for a specific initiative. Acting semi-independently from the main site, it caters to a niche audience or user need that benefits from a narrowed scope and tailored options, away from the broader offerings of the main site.

The microsite adopts the fundamental branding guidelines of ACC.org while introducing its unique design, structure, and organization. This concentration of tailored information and user actions makes it an effective tool for engagement.

    Pros of Microsites:

      >>>>>>> origin/main
    1. Focused Content: Microsites allow you to create content that is highly targeted towards a specific audience or topic. This can help improve user engagement and conversion rates for that specific topic or audience.
    2. Product Brand Alterations: Microsites provide an opportunity to apply branding and design strategies unique to the product.
    3. Campaign-Specific Messaging: Microsites can be an effective tool for unique marketing campaigns or product launches, where a dedicated site can deliver a focused message.
    4. Fast Deployment: Microsites can typically be augmented and launched more quickly than adding substantial new sections to an existing site. Most often this work is bespoke and one off.
    5. Flexibility: Microsites can be easily tweaked or shut down without affecting the rest of the company's web presence.
    6. User Experience: Microsites can offer a more streamlined and targeted user experience, leading to improved customer satisfaction and conversion rates for a product.
    7. Improved Tracking: It may be easier to track the effectiveness of a specific campaign or audience through a microsite, as analytics can be specifically tailored for the site.
    8. Independence: If different departments or branches of a company want to maintain a certain level of autonomy in their web presence, microsites can offer this independence.

    The Negative Costs of a Microsite

    While microsites can be valuable tools, they also come with inherent challenges that can affect your digital strategy. These challenges underline the fact that while microsites can be effective for specific initiatives, they should be used judiciously and with clear purpose, given the potential costs involved.

    1. Brand Consistency: Operating multiple microsites can disrupt brand uniformity and recognition, causing customer confusion.
    2. Increased Management Complexity: The operation and maintenance of various microsites amplify the complexity of website management, requiring additional resources. The bespoke nature of the code might also lead to problems during system upgrades.
    3. SEO Risks: Microsites could cannibalize the SEO value of the main site and end up competing with it in search results.
      1. User Navigation and Context Confusion: Due to their distinctive branding and self-contained nature, microsites can create navigation challenges for users. Moving between multiple microsites and the main site can lead to a disorienting user experience. This context-switching confusion is typically less prevalent in a single, unified site of the main site.
    5. Content Duplication: Managing content across the main sites and isolated microsites introduces the risk of duplication and subsequent issues with content upkeep, maintenance and SEO.
    6. Traffic Dilution: Traffic is dispersed across multiple microsites instead of being directed to one central site, leading to a dilution of traffic on the main site and less cross-site discovery.
    7. Discoverability: If not directly linked or referenced from the main site, microsites may be more difficult for users to find.
    8. Sustainability: Maintaining and updating numerous microsites over time can prove challenging, potentially resulting in outdated information.
