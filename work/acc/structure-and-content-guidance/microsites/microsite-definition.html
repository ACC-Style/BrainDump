Microsite Definition
date created
Jul 18 2023
date updated
Jul 18 2023 @ 10:34:32-04:00

A microsite, also termed a landing page or brochure pages, is an independent yet integrative subsection within the main ACC.org framework. Though this division bears the basic branding of the main site, it introduces its unique sub-branding elements. These components establish a distinct identity, subtly differentiating the microsite while preserving coherence with the main brand. The structure of the microsite comprises multiple interconnected pages that form an exclusive navigation tree. This enclosed network ensures that each page links exclusively to other pages within the microsite, creating a tailored, immersive user journey. With a clearly defined central focus and goal, the microsite serves as a concentrated platform for strategic communication and user interaction.

TLDR Quick Read

  1. Microsite: A distinct, yet cohesive, self-contained subsection within the ACC.org site.
  2. Sub Branding Elements: Unique branding features that distinguish the microsite from the main ACC.org brand, yet align with its basic branding.
  3. Enclosed Navigation Tree: The microsite encompasses a network of pages that interlink exclusively within its own scope, creating a customized navigation experience.
  4. Central Focus and Goal: The microsite is crafted with a specific objective, functioning as a dedicated communication and engagement platform.
  5. Aliases: The microsite is also known as Landing Page and Brochure Pages.
