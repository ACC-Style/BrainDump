The Company

The American College of Cardiology (ACC) is a US-based global nonprofit medical society dedicated to transforming cardiovascular care and improving heart health. Founded in 1949, it serves as a professional home for cardiovascular clinicians by advancing education, clinical standards, and policy initiatives while also leading efforts in quality improvement, advocacy, and research dissemination.

Membership and Users of the Products

ACC’s membership includes more than 56,000 professionals worldwide, representing a diverse mix of cardiologists, cardiovascular surgeons, nurses, physician assistants, pharmacists, researchers, and other health professionals. Members span across all career stages, from medical students and trainees to senior practitioners and international experts. This broad member base fosters collaboration, mentorship, and the exchange of knowledge across the spectrum of cardiovascular medicine.

Product Owner (EDCS)

A Product Owner in EDCS defines the vision, priorities, and roadmap for digital products. They manage backlogs, translate business goals into actionable work, and ensure delivery aligns with both user needs and organizational strategy. Acting as the bridge between stakeholders, developers, and designers, they represent the customer voice while ensuring measurable outcomes. Within ACC, Product Owners work closely with UX Designers, MarCom, and IT to deliver consistent, value-driven digital experiences that reflect both user requirements and enterprise goals.

UX Designer (EDCS)

A UX Designer in EDCS ensures digital experiences are accessible, clear, and user-focused. They conduct research, map journeys, and create prototypes or wireframes to streamline usability. UX Designers balance user needs with business objectives and work closely with Product Owners to ensure products meet goals. Their designs apply Arches Patterns consistently, creating repeatable experiences across platforms. At ACC, UX is central to making digital tools intuitive while ensuring the broader MarCom brand voice is faithfully translated into functional, engaging designs.

Web Production (Sitecore/Sitefinity)

Web Production staff manage page creation and updates in Sitecore or Sitefinity. With basic HTML knowledge and Arches Pattern usage, their work emphasizes accuracy, repeatability, and consistency. They implement content and design direction provided by EDCS and MarCom while ensuring CMS workflows remain efficient and reliable. Web Producers handle formatting, publishing, and troubleshooting common issues. Serving as the operational layer of digital delivery, they translate strategy, design, and content decisions into live experiences that remain on-brand and technically stable.

Business Line (BL)

Business Lines (BLs) represent core ACC departments such as Education, Science and Quality, or Membership. Each BL manages its portfolio of programs, services, and products, setting priorities and outcomes while collaborating with EDCS, MarCom, Web Production, and IT. BLs ensure offerings remain financially sustainable, strategically aligned, and meaningful to members. Their decisions provide direction on what needs to be built or updated, while other divisions ensure execution. This structure enables BLs to focus on subject matter expertise while leveraging enterprise teams for delivery.

MarCom (Marketing and Communications)

MarCom shapes the voice, brand, and visual identity of the ACC. They provide final approval on text, tone, and graphics across campaigns and digital properties, ensuring consistency and authority. MarCom defines the broad brand direction and messaging, while EDCS translates it into digital execution. Their oversight ensures that outputs are polished, professional, and aligned with the ACC’s mission. In partnership with BLs, Web Production, and IT, MarCom ensures that every communication—whether print or digital—reinforces the college’s trusted, global brand identity.

IT (Technology and Development)

The IT division brings together developers across back-end, front-end, and full-stack disciplines to design, build, and maintain ACC’s digital infrastructure. This team ensures platforms like Sitecore, Sitefinity, and custom systems are stable, scalable, and secure. IT works closely with EDCS and Web Production to implement features, troubleshoot issues, and deliver technical enhancements. Specialized developers also support integration with external systems and performance optimization. As the technical foundation of digital projects, IT enables innovation while ensuring compliance, reliability, and long-term sustainability of ACC’s platforms.

Web Domains

  • ACC.org is the official website of the American College of Cardiology. It serves as a comprehensive resource for cardiology professionals, offering a wide range of content including clinical updates, educational materials, guidelines, and information on cardiology conferences and events. The site is designed to support medical professionals in the field of cardiology with the latest scientific research, policy advocacy, and continuous education. It also provides tools and resources for practice support, quality improvement, and patient care in the cardiovascular field. The website caters to the needs of cardiologists, providing them with essential information to advance their knowledge and practice. This Domain is built on the CMS of Sitecore.
  • CardioSmart.org is a patient-centered website created by the American College of Cardiology. It provides comprehensive information about heart health and various cardiac conditions. The site is dedicated to educating patients, offering resources on heart diseases, healthy living tips, and tools to manage cardiac health. It includes topics such as heart disease prevention, treatment options, and lifestyle changes. CardioSmart.org is designed to empower patients with knowledge and tools to actively participate in their own heart care and make informed decisions about their health. This Domain is built on the CMS of SiteFinity.
  • CVQuality.acc.org serves as a hub for the ACC Quality Improvement for Institutions Program, catering primarily to healthcare professionals who focus on quality improvements and administration rather than direct clinical practice. It offers resources and initiatives like NCDR registries, accreditation services, and clinical toolkits aimed at enhancing cardiovascular care quality. The site provides valuable support and tools for healthcare facilities to implement evidence-based practices and engage in quality improvement projects, aligning with the administrative and quality improvement aspects of healthcare.
  • https://www.jacc.org/ The Journal of the American College of Cardiology (JACC) is the flagship peer-reviewed journal of the ACC and one of the most influential publications in cardiovascular medicine. Ranked among the top journals in the field, JACC publishes cutting-edge research, clinical trials, expert reviews, and practice guidelines that shape cardiovascular practice worldwide. It is part of the broader JACC Family of Journals, which cover subspecialties such as heart failure, imaging, intervention, electrophysiology, and basic science.

Technical Project

  • ARCHES is a comprehensive front-end design system by the American College of Cardiology. It uses advanced compiler techniques to generate variations that integrate with different frameworks and brands, making it versatile for various ACC products. ARCHES primarily consists of utility classes that offer a modern approach to CSS, focusing on single-style control and separation of responsibilities between HTML and CSS. This system is flexible and adaptable, used across multiple ACC platforms, including ACC.org and CardioSmart, and supports distinct brand styles while maintaining overall coherence. This solution uses NPM, SCSS, and KSS framework to produce the documentation which is hosted here https://assets.acc.org/Arches/Latest/docs/.
  • ARCHES Bespoke is a set of micro style sheets when a solution is very specialized and the styles are not appropriate to add to the overhead of all pages and it is need for a focused specialized usage.
  • PaperCrane refers to a Component Library specifically designed for Vue.js, a progressive JavaScript framework. This library is utilized in the development of Single Page Applications (SPAs). PaperCrane contains a collection of pre-built Vue components which can be reused across different parts of the enterprise application, enhancing development speed and ensuring UI consistency. This project is built with Storybook and is hosted here https://assets.acc.org/papercrane/Latest/docs/?path=/story/paper-crane--page
  • ONECRM is a an effort to move our CRM(Customer Relationship Management Software) from Personify to Salesforce. This is a multi year effort and is complex collaboration of setting up Salesforce to fit the needs of ACC. It is also an effort of updating API, User Experience Flows, Integrations, Solutions, and Content across our digital platforms. Products that are majorly effected is the Shopping Cart that used to be Ebiz now Salesforce. Membership Application from OMA to Salesforce, and Membership Management.
  • HubSpot is a email services. The HubSpot platform is starting to be positioned to house "Marketing Landing Pages". These pages are are directly connected to a marketing campaign, they do not have extensive connections or navigation paths from any of our Domains, and they are also time box and have a limited lifespan.
