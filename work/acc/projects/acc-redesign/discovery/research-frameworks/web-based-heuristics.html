Web Based Heuristics

(Based on NN Group)

1. Clear Feedback on User Actions

  • Ensure the site provides immediate feedback for user actions, such as logging in, submitting forms, or loading pages, so users know their actions are being processed.

2. Use Familiar and Understandable Language

  • The content and navigation should use terms and language that resonate with your audience (e.g., "My Courses," "Membership Dashboard," "Explore Articles") to make the site intuitive.

3. Allow Easy Navigation and Undo Options

  • Users should be able to easily navigate between sections, undo actions, and go back without confusion, especially when managing membership details or course enrollments.

4. Maintain Consistent Design and Navigation

  • The design, layout, and labeling should remain consistent across the site, whether users are in the membership area, reading articles, or accessing educational content.

5. Prevent Errors with Clear Instructions

  • Provide users with clear, guided steps to minimize mistakes, particularly in processes like signing up for a membership, enrolling in a course, or making a purchase.

6. Make Important Information Easy to Find

  • Reduce the need for users to remember details by displaying key information clearly, such as membership status, progress in courses, and recommended articles.

7. Cater to Different Experience Levels

  • Design for both new and experienced users, allowing shortcuts and advanced features (like filtering or bookmarking) for members who visit frequently while keeping it simple for new visitors.

8. Keep Design Clean and Focused

  • Ensure that pages are visually simple and easy to navigate, focusing on the most important actions like reading articles, accessing courses, or checking membership benefits.

9. Provide Clear Error Messages and Solutions

  • If something goes wrong, provide a clear explanation and actionable steps to resolve the issue, such as payment problems or content loading errors.

10. Make Help Resources Easily Accessible

  • Ensure that users can quickly find help through FAQs, support articles, or a contact option, especially when they are having trouble with their membership, accessing courses, or reading content.
