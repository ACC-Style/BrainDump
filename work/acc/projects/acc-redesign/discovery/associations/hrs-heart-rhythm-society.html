Main Site vs Journal Site Comparison (HRS)

Explanation:
The Heart Rhythm Journal is completely separate from the HRS website:

  • It operates on an entirely different domain (heartrhythmjournal.com), run through Elsevier.
  • Branding, navigation, and user journeys are completely siloed — there are no obvious cross-links back to the HRS main site, nor any integrated experience for HRS members.
  • Full journal access flows externally; it feels like an independent publishing platform rather than an extension of the HRS organization.
  • Content is distinctly scientific and does not overlap with HRS’s broader event, patient, or professional education materials.
  • The +.5 is because there is a decent amount of marketing effort on the pages to direct users to the journals.
  • +1 the branding of the journal site has echos and is very clear that the Journal is produced by the HRS.
    Thus, this pairing is disconnected with connective DNA with very low/no overlap in content.

Membership Experience

Explanation:
The majority of event summaries, patient tools, and basic professional resources are freely accessible. Deeper educational materials, full conference recordings, and certain guidelines are gated for members. There's a relatively friendly and proactive onboarding pathway — clear "Join Now" CTAs, member value propositions, and defined next steps post-registration.

Brand Personality

The primary audience is still electrophysiologists and cardiac device professionals, making the experience somewhat insider-focused. However, the tone of writing is noticeably friendlier and more humanized compared to peers like HFSA. The voice is professional without being overly dry, especially in patient-facing pages and public health initiatives.
