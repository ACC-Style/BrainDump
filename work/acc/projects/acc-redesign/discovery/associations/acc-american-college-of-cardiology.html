Main Site vs Journal Site

Explanation

The main site (ACC.org) and the journal site (JACC.org) are distinct in branding and navigation. While ACC.org provides a link to JACC.org, the journal site operates independently with its own navigation and content structure. The content overlap is moderate; ACC.org references JACC articles, but the journal content is primarily housed separately.

Effect on UX

A very disjunctive experience between the two space but with all of the interconnected hand offs between the two sites and the non-descriptive branding the experience comes out muddled and users will often not understand they a have left one domain for the other.

Membership Experience

Explanation

ACC.org offers a mix of open-access and member-restricted content. Educational resources and certain tools require membership login. The onboarding process is streamlined, with clear pathways for different professional roles and an eligibility calculator for prospective members.

Effect on UX

The site largely seems unlocked and most articles and site are unlocked thought it makes easy access to the site and the content produced by the org it lacks a certain presentation to the value of Membership or what a user might get to if the content was more locked down.

Brand Personality

Explanation

The site primarily targets cardiovascular professionals, with content tailored to their needs. The tone is formal and clinical, appropriate for a professional medical audience, but lacks broader inclusivity or expressive engagement visitors. Most language lacks pro-nouns, and mostly proper nouns. This language is professional but doesn't match to modern web language.

Effect on UX the tone can come across as overly wordy to cold. The text is to long to be skimmed and often just produces long unreadable blocks of text.
