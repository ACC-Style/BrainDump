Main Site vs. Journal Site

Integration of Experience (X-Axis)

Rationale: The AMA's main site (ama-assn.org) and the JAMA Network (jamanetwork.com) operate on separate domains with distinct branding and navigation structures. Minimal links between the two—such as the AMA site directing users to the JAMA Network for publications—the user experience is disjointed. Users must navigate between different interfaces, and there is no unified account system or integrated search functionality across both platforms. This separation indicates a loosely connected integration of experience.​

  • No logo Sharing
  • No Design Sharing
  • Barely Spells out JAMA
  • Science Only

Content Overlap (Y-Axis)

Rationale: The content between the AMA main site and the JAMA Network is largely distinct. The AMA site focuses on advocacy, medical education, and professional resources, while the JAMA Network is dedicated to publishing peer-reviewed medical research. Although both platforms serve medical professionals and are under the AMA umbrella, their content offerings do not significantly overlap, indicating a low content overlap.​

Membership Experience

Rationale: The AMA offers certain resources, such as CME options and articles, to non-members, but full access to benefits like the JAMA Network and specific discounts requires membership. The onboarding process is streamlined, with clear pathways for different user groups (e.g., physicians, residents, students) to understand and access membership benefits.​American Medical Association
