Step 2: Concept Structure Glossary (with definitions)

Home Page

  • Definition: The main entry point of the website, designed to provide immediate orientation, highlight major initiatives, latest updates, and guide users quickly toward primary goals.
  • Use Case: First-time visitors, returning users seeking major updates, initial brand impression.
  • Why Used: Creates immediate clarity and positions organizational priorities upfront.
  • Branding & UI Elements: Take over, Hero Image, Feature Triptych...

Concept Anchors

  • Definition: Pages offering high-level overviews of the organization's core concepts, primary service areas, or central offerings. They orient users by clearly presenting a broader narrative or theme.

  • Example Page: About US

  • Use Case: Navigation entry points for broad organizational topics, connecting various subpages and initiatives.

  • Why Used: Humanizes the organization, reinforces brand values, and simplifies access to deeper content.

  • Branding & UI Elements: Concept Hero with Feature, Grid Bento, UL Icon Text List of Icon ULs, Grouping Zones, Section Shading

Initiatives (Goal-Oriented Modules)

  • Definition: Targeted microsites or sections devoted to specific organizational efforts or campaigns, often incorporating unique branding or visual differentiation. These modules are action-focused, with clearly defined user goals.
  • Example Page: Advocacy | UX
  • Use Case: Ded. cated fundraising campaigns, advocacy efforts, specialized educational content, or strategic programs needing distinct identity and messaging.
  • Why Used: Enhances clarity of purpose, supports targeted user journeys, simplifies complex or strategic messaging.
  • Branding & UI Elements: Microsite Hero Banner, Secondary Action Card, Cards, Icon & Content Grid List, Grouping Zones, Section Shading

Product Groupings

  • Definition: Pages that consolidate and categorize related products, services, or offerings into logical collections. These pages help users quickly explore and compare similar or complementary options.
  • Example Page: Prototype SAPs
  • Use Case: Showcasing related educational programs, courses, events, or membership offerings to simplify discovery.
  • Why Used: Improves usability, encourages product exploration, and enhances contextual understanding of offerings.
  • Branding & UI Elements: Product Banner, Icon & Content Grid List, Cards, Grouping Zones

Product Destinations (Transactional Pages)

  • Definition: Detailed, user-action oriented pages centered around specific products, events, or learning opportunities. These pages incorporate transaction-focused elements such as pricing, purchasing options, registration, personalization, and targeted marketing content.
  • Use Case: Event registrations, online courses, digital product sales, personalized service subscriptions.
  • Why Used: Maximizes conversions, streamlines user actions, integrates closely with marketing and sales funnels.
  • Branding & UI Elements: Product Banner, Conversion Buttons, Price Bentos, Icon & Content Grid List, Cards, Grouping Zones

Standard Pages

  • Definition: Basic informational pages that deliver foundational content. These pages rely on simplicity, clarity, and consistency, typically inheriting brand or stylistic elements from parent sections.
  • Use Case: General information pages, FAQ pages, policy statements, foundational details, or general descriptions.
  • Why Used: Ensures content consistency, simplifies content maintenance, provides informational clarity without complexity.
  • Branding & UI Elements: Basic Html, Accordions, Inline Images

Listing Pages

  • Definition: Pages built around dynamically generated collections or search-driven lists, organized by shared attributes (such as product type, topic, date, popularity). They are designed for easy browsing and discovery.
  • Use Case: News archives, galleries, event listings, search results, product catalogs.
  • Why Used: Supports user exploration, content discoverability, and facilitates easy navigation of large sets of information.
  • Branding & UI Elements: Search Features, Result renderings, Group Zones

Logic & Rules for Using Each Page Type

â‘  Home Page

Role in IA:

  • The central entry point.
  • Highest-level orientation, connecting users quickly and intuitively to key site sections.
  • Prioritizes major pathways, brand introduction, and timely highlights.

Rules & Recommendations:

  • Use clearly defined navigation to major Concept Anchors and prioritized user tasks.
  • Limit content to essential items for quick user orientation (less is more).
  • Regularly update to reflect organizational priorities and timely initiatives.

When to Use:

  • Always present as the top-level entry page.

When to Avoid:

  • Do not overload with detailed content or overly transactional messaging.

â‘¡ Concept Anchors

Role in IA:

  • Provide clear, user-friendly overviews for broad organizational pillars or content areas.
  • Act as foundational navigational gateways to deeper, focused content.

Rules & Recommendations:

  • Clearly articulate the broader concept or brand narrative.
  • Include links to associated Initiatives, Product Groupings, or deeper Standard Pages.
  • Consistently reflect organizational brand and tone.

When to Use:

  • For core content areas users must quickly recognize and access.
  • When multiple related initiatives or products are associated with a central idea.

When to Avoid:

  • Do not use for single-topic, transactional, or overly detailed content.

â‘¢ Initiatives (Goal-Oriented Modules)

Role in IA:

  • Deeply focused content modules addressing specific user objectives.
  • Clear calls to action, distinct messaging, and optionally unique branding.

Rules & Recommendations:

  • Each initiative clearly states a purpose and expected user outcome.
  • Can have unique visual branding, differentiating from standard organizational branding.
  • May link directly from Concept Anchors or the Home Page for visibility.

When to Use:

  • Specific advocacy campaigns, fundraising, accreditation processes, or specialized content with unique user journeys.

When to Avoid:

  • Avoid if the content is purely informational or lacks a specific user goal or call to action.

â‘£ Product Groupings

Role in IA:

  • Organize related products or offerings to enhance discoverability.
  • Act as a navigational aid to guide users toward relevant transactional or informational content.

Rules & Recommendations:

  • Clearly grouped content based on user mental models or marketing strategy.
  • Ensure clear, logical connections to detailed transactional pages (Product Destinations).
  • Include brief summaries or value propositions to support product exploration.

When to Use:

  • Multiple offerings (courses, events, services) logically grouped by category, target audience, or usage.

When to Avoid:

  • Do not use for unrelated, singular, or heavily transactional content without logical groupings.

â‘¤ Product Destinations (Transactional Pages)

Role in IA:

  • Detail-focused pages directly supporting user conversion actions.
  • Facilitate seamless transactions (registration, purchase, enrollment).

Rules & Recommendations:

  • Must clearly articulate benefits, pricing, and actionable steps.
  • Integrate branding and marketing language specific to the product or service.
  • Maintain tight focus on conversion objectivesâ€”minimal distractions or unrelated links.

When to Use:

  • Directly linked from Product Groupings, marketing campaigns, or event promotions.

When to Avoid:

  • Do not use for general informational content lacking transactional intent.

â‘¥ Standard Pages

Role in IA:

  • Provide straightforward informational content, leveraging clarity and simplicity.
  • Maintain cohesion with broader parent-section branding.

Rules & Recommendations:

  • Keep clear, concise, and structured consistently across the site.
  • Use standardized navigation and minimalistic design approaches to avoid cognitive load.
  • Primarily inherit visual elements from their parent (Concept Anchors, Initiatives, or Product Groupings).

When to Use:

  • Informational pages (FAQs, About Us, Terms, or Policy content) that do not require unique user journeys.

When to Avoid:

  • Avoid using for highly transactional or uniquely branded content that needs distinctive visual differentiation.

â‘¦ Listing Pages

Role in IA:

  • Support exploration and discovery through dynamically generated or searchable lists.
  • Facilitate user-driven browsing or filtering actions.

Rules & Recommendations:

  • Clearly structured and sortable/searchable.
  • Ensure consistency in content presentation (titles, thumbnails, brief descriptions).
  • Provide clear pathways to deeper individual content items.

When to Use:

  • News feeds, galleries, product catalogs, resource libraries, event calendars, or any frequently updated content collections.

When to Avoid:

  • Do not use for single-instance content, detailed descriptions, or conversion-driven transactional content.
